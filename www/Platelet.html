Platelets

Did you know that...

Platelets are cell fragments produced from megakaryocytes. These polyploid (128n) cells in the bone marrow send pseudopodia-like projections into the lumen of adjacent blood vessels. Blood flowing through the vessel shears off the platelets.



Additional Information

Related Disease

Dengue, also known as break bone fever, due to the extreme pain caused in bones and muscles, is a mosquito-borne infection transmitted through the bite of an infected female Aedes aegypti mosquito.

The characteristic symptoms of dengue include:

  • Sudden-onset of fever (40C/ 104F). Fever is usually biphasic in nature, breaking and then returning for one or two days
  • Headache, pain behind the eyes
  • Muscle and joint pains
  • Swollen glands or skin rash that is similar to measles
  • Nausea, vomiting
