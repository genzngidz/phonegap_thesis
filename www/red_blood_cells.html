Red Blood Cells (Erythrocytes)

Did you know that...

Red blood cells primarily carry oxygen from the lungs to the cells of tissues and organs all around the body. In mammals, red blood cells contain hemoglobin, which is rich in iron, and gives blood it's red colour. There are approximately 5000 billion red blood cells per litre of blood and last about 120 days in the circulation. They don't have a nucleus (nucleated red blood cells are called reticulocytes) and are bi-concave in shape.



Additional Information

TRIVIA

  • Every day your body produces 100 billion new red blood cells. That's an ounce of blood, by the way. (You produce 2 million per second, we are told.)
  • Baby red blood cells are blue.
  • Women have a lower red blood cell count than men do.
