Blood

  • Blood Cells

    There are 3 blood cell types:
    Red blood cells (erythrocytes)
    White blood cells (Leukocytes)
    Platelets (thrombocytes).

  • Red Blood Cells (Erythrocytes)

    Red blood cells primarily carry oxygen from the lungs to the cells
    of tissues and organs all around the body. In mammals, red blood cells
    contain haemoglobin, which is rich in iron, and gives blood it’s red colour.

  • White Blood Cells (Leukocytes)

    White blood cells are active in defending the host from
    infections and other foreign bodies in the blood and tissues.

  • Platelets

    Platelets are cell fragments produced from megakaryocytes.
    These polyploid (128n) cells in the bone marrow send
    pseudopodia-like projections into the lumen of adjacent
    blood vessels. Blood flowing through the vessel shears
    off the platelets.

  • Plasma

    Plasma is a yellowish liquid that is mostly water.
    But it also carries important nutrients, hormones
    and proteins throughout the body.
