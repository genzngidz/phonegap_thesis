White Blood Cells (Leukocytes)

Did you know that...

White blood cells are active in defending the host from infections and other foreign bodies in the blood and tissues. There are in much smaller concentrations in the blood, only 5 billion per litre, but their numbers increase dramatically at the start of an infection. As they are produced so rapidly in a healthy person, there is no need to transfuse them.



Additional Information

Related Disease

Leukemia, also called blood cancer, is a group of diseases that is caused due to increased number of blood cells. Uncontrolled growth of any blood cell leads to leukemia. Most forms of the disease are caused due to high white blood cell count.

The following is a list of different types of leukemia that occur due to increased white blood:

  • Acute myeloid leukemia (AML)
  • Chronic myeloid leukemia (CML)
  • Acute lymphocytic leukemia (ALL)
  • Acute lymphocytic leukemia (ALL)
