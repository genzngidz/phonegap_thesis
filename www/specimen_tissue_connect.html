Connective Tissue

Did you know that...

The most abundant and the most widely distributed of the tissues. Connective tissues perform a variety of functions including support and protection. The following tissues are found in the human body, ordinary loose connective tissue, fat tissue, dense fibrous tissue, cartilage, bone, blood, and lymph, which are all considered connective tissue.



Additional Information


Read More...



The older classification of connective tissue had two subtypes:

  • Proper, which covered areolar and fibrous tissue;
  • Specialized, which included bone, blood, cartilage, adipose (fat) tissue, and reticular tissue.

    • The newer classification has four categories:
    • Loose Connective Tissue
    • Dense Connective Tissue
    • Cartilage
    • Other
